One summer while in college, I interviewed my grandpa about his life. That conversation helped me understand my family history in ways I never expected.

I saw how events decades ago shaped my family — my grandpa's experiences passed on to my mother and then to me. His life brimmed with fascinating details I never would've discovered if I hadn't taken the time to listen.

That's why I started Franklin Life Scribe — a life story book service dedicated to helping people preserve their stories for posterity. I'll interview you, weave your stories into a cohesive narrative, work with you to edit until it captures your voice, then professionally format and print your book.

No AI-generated content. Ever. This is your story, written the way only a human listener can write it.

Frequently asked questions.

My dad has a saying: "Everyone is fascinating." This has proved true again and again. Even people who say "I haven't done much with my life" turn out to be remarkable once you dig a little deeper. One storyteller told me he'd lived a good life but didn't know if there was much to tell — then casually mentioned he'd once been paralyzed from the neck down playing football, and recovered. That story of perseverance, peace, and resilience is exactly the kind of timeless wisdom your family deserves to inherit. Live long enough, and you will have stories of enduring hard things. Those stories matter, especially to your descendants.
For the storyteller, getting their story written is as easy as having a conversation. I'll guide you through five interview sessions, each just over an hour long. I listen attentively, ask thought-provoking questions, and help you discover themes in your life that you'd like to emphasize. Based on those interviews, I'll write a book that shows your life the way you want it told. Then we'll revise closely so it truly captures your voice. Family members mentioned by name will have a chance to read their parts. Once the final text is settled, I format the book, design a cover, and get it professionally printed.
Each storyteller is unique, and some want longer and more in-depth projects than others. Because of this, pricing varies. During my first — and free — meeting, I'll work with you to develop an outline for your book. After that conversation, I'll determine the cost. Pricing comes as a single flat rate, with 50% paid up-front and 50% upon completion.
As many as you want. I'll cover printing costs for up to one copy, and I'll send you the interior and cover design files. With those files in hand, printing additional copies through an online small-run printer like Lulu or IngramSpark is straightforward — and I'm happy to walk you through the process if you'd like.
Absolutely. A life story book makes a deeply meaningful gift. If you have an older person in your life who means a lot to you and whom you'd like to know better, I can help you give them — and your whole family — the gift of their preserved story. We can arrange the interviews around their schedule and comfort level.
